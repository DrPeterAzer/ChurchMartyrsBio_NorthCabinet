القديس العظيم الشهيد مارجرجس الروماني

The Great Martyr Saint George

(١ مايو / ٢٣ برمودة — 1 May / 23 Bramouda)

St. George the Roman is often seen as the prince of martyrs during the reign of Emperor Diocletian, as he led a revolutionary movement in Cappadocia against the decree of Christian persecution. However, most Coptic manuscripts suggest he lived earlier, during the reign of an illegitimate king named Dadianus the Persian, a pagan ruler...

His Early Life: Saint George was born in Cappadocia to wealthy and devout parents...

Prince George the Roman: After his father’s martyrdom, Prince Justus succeeded him and took care of George’s education...

The Governor’s Affection: Justus desired to marry George to his only daughter...

His Zeal for the Faith: George learned that the emperor had issued a decree...

Before the Emperor: George was summoned before the emperor, who tried to bribe and flatter him...

Stronger than Magic and Poison: The emperor brought in a sorcerer to poison George...

In the Pagan Temple: The emperor brought George to a temple hoping he'd worship idols...

Victory Over Temptation: Hoping to break George through lust, a seductive woman was sent...

In the Royal Palace: The queen overheard George’s prayers and converted to Christianity...

His Martyrdom: Fearing rebellion due to the many miracles through George, the emperor finally ordered his beheading...
